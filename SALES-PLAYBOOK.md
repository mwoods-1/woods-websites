Reference guide for pricing, selling, and delivering projects.

## Pricing

### Upfront (project fee)

| Project type | Price range | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Simple brochure site (3-5 pages) | $500 – $1,000 | Design, build, mobile responsive, basic SEO |
| Business site with CMS | $1,000 – $2,000 | Above + client can edit content themselves |
| Site + booking/payments | $2,000 – $3,000 | Above + Stripe, scheduling, automation |
| Custom software/portal | $3,000+ | Tailored tools, dashboards, integrations |

### Monthly retainer

| Complexity | Retainer |
|---|---|
| Simple brochure site | $50/month |
| Business site with CMS | $75 – $100/month |
| Site + integrations/software | $100 – $150/month |

**Tip:** Say "$25 a week" instead of "$100/month" — sounds smaller, same money.

### Payment structure

- 50% upfront before any work starts
- 50% on completion before the site goes live
- Monthly retainer charged via Stripe subscription (auto-billing)

## Delivery timeline

### Actual build time (don't tell clients this)

- Simple site: 3 – 8 hours
- Site with CMS/booking: 10 – 20 hours
- Custom software: 20 – 40+ hours

### What to tell clients

- Simple site: 1 – 2 weeks
- Site with CMS/booking: 2 – 3 weeks
- Custom software: 3 – 6 weeks

Always pad the timeline. Under-promise, over-deliver.

## The sales process

1. **Lead comes in** — referral, cold approach, website enquiry, business card
2. **Free discovery call** (15-30 min) — what's your business, what do you need, what are your goals?
3. **Send a proposal** — scope, price, timeline (keep to 1 page)
4. **50% deposit** before any work starts
5. **Build it** — share progress at halfway point for feedback
6. **Client review** — one round of revisions included
7. **Final 50% payment** before site goes live
8. **Launch** — deploy, domain, hand over CMS access
9. **Monthly retainer** starts — hosting, support, minor updates

## How to say the price

> "Based on what we've discussed, this would be around $X — that includes the design, build, mobile optimisation, and a month of support after launch."

Then stop talking. Don't justify, don't apologise, don't offer a discount before they react.

If they say it's too much:

> "I get it — what's your budget? I can adjust the scope to fit."

Cut features, not your rate. Fewer pages, simpler design, no CMS. Never do the same work for less money.

## You vs Squarespace/Wix

This will come up. Here's your answer:

| | Squarespace/Wix | You |
|---|---|---|
| They build it themselves | Yes (20-40 hours) | No — you handle everything |
| Looks like a template | Yes | No — custom to their brand |
| Performance & SEO | Bloated, average | Fast, optimised, ranks |
| Custom features | Limited plugins | Built exactly how they need |
| Ongoing cost | $25-50/month + their time | $25/week, you handle it all |
| Something breaks | YouTube and support tickets | They text you, it's fixed |

**The line:**

> "You could build it yourself on Squarespace — but you'd spend 30+ hours on something that still looks like a template and doesn't rank on Google. I handle everything, it's custom to your business, and when you need a change you just message me. The $25 a week is less than you'd spend on coffee."

## Finding clients (ranked by ROI)

### Do this week
- **Ask past clients for referrals** — text or call Aviation Expeditions, Sven's Basecamp, Ovens Soccer, Kingdom Property Care
- **Ask mates and family** — "know any business owners who need a website?"
- **Set up Google Business Profile** — free, shows up in "web developer near me" searches

### Ongoing
- **Facebook/LinkedIn local business groups** — be helpful, answer questions, post case studies
- **Walk into local businesses** — cafes, gyms, tradies with bad websites, hand them a card
- **Cold email/DM** — personalised, mention something specific about their current site

### Skip for now
- Paid ads (no budget to test, Calendly not set up yet)
- Blog content (takes months to rank)
- Fiverr/Upwork (race to the bottom)

## Handling the fear

- Nobody cares about you as much as you think. A "no" is rejecting the timing, not you.
- 10 conversations = 2-3 interested leads = 1 paying client. That's normal.
- You already have 4 clients on your portfolio. This works.
- Undercharging makes clients trust you less. The price is a trust signal.
- A $2,000 website that brings a business 2 extra customers/month pays for itself month one.
